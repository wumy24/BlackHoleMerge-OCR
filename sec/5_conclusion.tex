  \section{Conclusion}
  \label{sec:conclusion}

  We present \ours{}, a content-adaptive visual token compression method for document understanding. \ours{} uses
  the L2 norm of Key vectors in Vision Transformers as an annotation-free content density signal. It preserves high-
  density tokens as absorbers and merges low-density tokens into semantically and spatially related absorber hidden
  states, reducing the visual sequence length while retaining useful information. To address the limitation that
  cross-entropy training tends to preserve more visual evidence and does not directly optimize compression, we adopt
  a two-stage training strategy. Merge-aware SFT first adapts the model to merged visual representations, and RL
  then trains a lightweight Policy MLP with an explicit quality-compression reward to learn content-adaptive keep/
  drop decisions.

  Experiments show that \ours{} dynamically adjusts compression strength according to the information density within
  each document page. It preserves more visual tokens in text-dense regions and applies stronger compression to
  blank or low-information regions. Compared with fixed-ratio compression and training-free token selection methods,
  \ours{} achieves a better balance between document parsing quality and inference efficiency.
