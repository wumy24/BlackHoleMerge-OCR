  \section{Conclusion}
  \label{sec:conclusion}

  We present \ours{}, a content-adaptive visual token compression method that uses
  the L2 norm of ViT Key vectors as an annotation-free content-density signal: it keeps high-density tokens as
  absorbers and merges low-density ones into semantically and spatially related absorbers,
  shortening the visual sequence while retaining useful context. Because cross-entropy training tends to
  preserve visual evidence rather than optimize compression, we combine merge-aware SFT with a lightweight RL
  policy that learns keep/drop decisions under an explicit quality--compression reward. Across
  multiple end-to-end OCR backbones, \ours{} adapts compression to per-page information density and achieves a better quality--efficiency
  balance than fixed-ratio compression and training-free token selection.
  A current limitation is that the density signal and merge boundary are learned per backbone; extending \ours{} to non-OCR tasks and jointly learning the merge layer are promising directions.
