  \section{Conclusion}
  \label{sec:conclusion}

  We present \ours{}, a content-adaptive visual token compression method for document understanding that uses
  the L2 norm of ViT Key vectors as an annotation-free content-density signal: it keeps high-density tokens as
  absorbers and merges low-density tokens into semantically and spatially related absorber hidden states,
  shortening the visual sequence while retaining useful information. Because cross-entropy training tends to
  preserve visual evidence rather than optimize compression, we combine merge-aware SFT with a lightweight RL
  policy that learns content-adaptive keep/drop decisions under an explicit quality--compression reward. Across
  multiple end-to-end OCR backbones, \ours{} adapts compression strength to per-page information density---preserving
  text-dense regions while aggressively compressing blank areas---and achieves a better balance between parsing
  quality and inference efficiency than fixed-ratio compression and training-free token selection.
  A current limitation is that the density signal and merge boundary are learned per backbone; extending \ours{}
  to a wider range of document VLMs and to non-OCR multimodal tasks, and jointly learning the merge layer rather
  than fixing it, are promising directions for future work.
